How do you measure influence?

Influence on Twitter can be elusive to define, especially given the limitations imposed by Twitter's API. Here's how our method works.

Each time you type in a hashtag, Twitter's API returns the 100 most popular tweets about the # based on Twitter's own algorithm. We rank the tweets based on the number of retweets they generate and display the top 5 tweets —— these are the big circles you see. For each top tweet, we rank its retweeters by looking at their follower numbers, and display the top 5 retweeters as the small circles within a big circles.

We acknowledge that our method is far from perfect, for two main reasons: First, Twitter's API doesn't count edited retweets, so those retweets don't show up in our visualization. Second, Twitter's API doesn't tratck the retweets of a retweet, so we evaluate the influence of the retweeters based on their follower numbers instead.
